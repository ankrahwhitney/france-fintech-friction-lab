Largest loss: **before document submission**. Sharpest segment gaps (diagnostic, not causal):
residence-permit holders fund at 8.5% vs 17.3% for national ID; unstable-network sessions fund at
11.1% with 12.2% support contact (`artifacts/segment_friction.csv`).

score selects what to *test*, not what to roll out (`docs/methodology.md`).

## Sensitivity

The checklist wins 4 of 5 weight profiles (Balanced, Growth first, Operations first, Evidence
first). **Speed first selects the explainer** (79.3 vs 77.7). If leadership optimises for the
fastest learning cycle, the recommendation changes — that dependency is surfaced, not hidden.

## The decision

Run a controlled test of the checklist: primary metric document submission (baseline 57.1%,
MDE 2.5 pp, 6,103 per arm, 12,206 total ≈ 37 days at 10,000 applications/month), funded-in-7-days
as directional secondary, guardrails on verification pass rate, manual review, support contact and
compliance indicators. Ship only if the primary metric clears the threshold with no guardrail
breach.
