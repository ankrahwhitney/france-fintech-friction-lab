# Strategy & Operations evidence map

This repository is deliberately organised around operating decisions, not a list of tools.

| Capability | Evidence | What it proves |
|---|---|---|
| Problem-solving at scale | 50,000-application funnel and event model | Can turn a broad growth problem into measurable stages and constraints |
| Customer and internal experience | Document loss, support contact and review-latency cuts | Looks at customer conversion and operational consequences together |
| Product/process development | Three scoped interventions with hypotheses and delivery estimates | Converts diagnosis into buildable options rather than stopping at analysis |
| Data-driven action | DuckDB SQL → scorecard → recommendation → experiment | Connects analysis to a specific next decision |
| Market and regulatory judgement | France research protocol with EBA, ANSSI, CNIL and ACPR anchors | Knows where public research ends and specialist review begins |
| Execution ownership | Named phases, owners, exit criteria, kill switches and reporting cadence | Plans how work moves from instrument to build, test and scale/stop |
| Communication | Executive memo, one-pager, dashboard and three-minute walkthrough | Adapts the same evidence to senior, operating and technical audiences |
| Analytical integrity | Claims register, fixed score anchors, data-quality report and explicit limitations | Makes uncertainty and model dependence auditable |

## What I would do with real company data

1. Align the event contract with Product, Data, Operations and Compliance.
2. Rebuild the funnel by country, flow version and verification path.
3. Quantify review-queue cost and downstream funding with observed cohorts.
4. Replace every authored lift, cost and confidence input with historical or researched evidence.
5. Pre-register the experiment and ship the smallest reversible intervention.
6. Report the result even if it disproves the preferred hypothesis.

The point of the case is not that a checklist is universally correct. It is that the repository
shows a fast, defensible way to decide what evidence to collect next.
